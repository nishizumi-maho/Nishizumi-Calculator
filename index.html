Drive Fair Share rule

All declared drivers must drive a “fair share” of the team’s laps. A fair share is defined as at least 25% of an equal share of laps. The equal share is the total laps divided by the number of declared drivers, rounded up to the next whole lap.

Example A

Total laps: 827 • Drivers: 6 • Equal share: 138 laps • Fair share: 35 laps.

Example B

Total laps: 500 • Drivers: 18 • Equal share: 28 laps • Fair share: 7 laps.
